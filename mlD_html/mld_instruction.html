Instruction

As a teacher with over 15 years of experience in the field of Chinese Medicine, Marshall has developed a methodology that incorporates not only Acupuncture (needling), but Tui Na based bodywork techniques that render an effective, holistic approach to healing and well-being. He places an importance on maintaining a relaxed, structurally aligned physical ability, utilizing underlying principles of Qi Gong to help prevent the fatigue that can overtime have a deleterious effect on a bodywork provider.

Marshall is a graduate of Tri-State College of Acupuncture (2002), where he is currently conducting Grand Rounds. In addition, Marshall is developing a Tui Na Bodywork curriculum that can be applied towards fulfilling continuing education unit (CEU's) requirements.

THE FIVE ELEMENT QI GONG (DEMONSTRATION VIDEO)

In the video, Marshall steps through the Five Element Qi Gong, demonstrating the techniques, while discussing the philosophy of this age-old system. The Five Elements (Metal, Water, Wood, Fire and Earth) correspond to the underlying energetics and functions of the organs systems, particularly as they pertain to Asian Medicine. Each exercise is structurally designed to affect the organ system to which element is associated with.

METAL: Lung / Large Intestine
  • Begin with the hands at the sides.
  • Lift the hands in front of the body to shoulder height, palms facing down with arms straight.
  • Move arms out to the sides of the body.
  • The palms should face up when the arms are fully extended.
  • Keeping the arms extended, close them in front of the body.
  • The palms should face down once the hands have reached the center of the body at shoulder height.
  • Repeat 10x at a slow to moderate pace.
    • WATER: Kidney / Urinary Bladder
  • Begin with the hands at the sides.
  • While turning the waist and shifting the weight to the left, place the back of the left hand on the right Kidney.
  • At the same time, scoop and circle upward with the right arm palm up, toward the left side; the navel should be facing approximately 45 degrees to the left.
  • As the hand reaches the level of the head (palm towards face) begin turning the waist and shifting the weight to the right side.
  • At the same time begin unwinding the right arm and hand in a circular motion to the level of the hip, palm down level with the forearm.
  • The hips should be turned to the right, with the navel orientated 45 degrees to the right.
  • Alternate right and left 10x each.

    WOOD: Liver / Gall Bladder
  • Begin with the feet shoulder width apart.
  • Extend the right arm shoulder high with the palm facing down approximately one hand width off the midline of the body.
  • The left hand is chambered palm up at the left hip with the elbow pointing straight back roughly belt level.
  • Retract the right hand while at the same time bringing forward the left hand.
  • By the time the hands pass one another on the midline, the left palm should be facing down and the right palm up, facing one another.
  • The body should slightly sink its center of gravity as the hands pass one another and slightly rise again, as the right hand is now chambered at the hip and the left is extended.
    		•
    FIRE: Heart / Small Intestine
  • Begin with hands in the lower basin position, palms up.
  • Shift the weight, turning the waist to the right, bringing the right arm up, turning the palm outward from the right corner of the head.
  • Think of opening the Small Intestine meridian.
  • At the same time the left hand crosses the body at heart level and presses to the right a few inches beyond the plane of the right palm.
  • The feeling is of the left side of the upper jio folding into the right.
  • The navel should be turned approximately 45 degrees to the right.
  • Return back to lower basin position and continue alternately right to left.
  • Repeat 10x.
    		•
    EARTH: Spleen / Stomach
  • Starting from the lower basin position, circle the arms above the head, palms facing upward.
  • The tips of the index fingers and thumbs should touch, forming a triangle.
  • While looking through the triangle, turn the upper torso from one side to the other.
  • Inhale in the central (neutral) position and exhale when turning to the side(s).
  • Repeat 10x.
